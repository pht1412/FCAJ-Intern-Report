During my internship at the **AWS Study Group VN / FirstCloudAIJourney** Bootcamp from **April 15, 2026** to **July 15, 2026**, I had the invaluable opportunity to hone my professional skills and prove myself in a high-pressure, real-world working environment.

In the "Mini Social Network" project, I took on the role of **Team Leader**, guiding a cross-functional team of 5 members (2 Developers, 1 Security Engineer, 2 Network Engineers). My responsibilities ranged from project management to hands-on technical execution, specifically:
*   Analyzing the project scope, breaking down complex tasks, and delegating them according to each member's specialized domain.
*   Hosting weekly progress meetings and synchronizing the entire team's workflow.
*   Actively participating in drawing the system architecture diagrams and configuring the CI/CD pipelines for both Frontend and Backend.
*   Upgrading the deployment process: Leading the transition from manual operations (ClickOps) to Infrastructure as Code (IaC - CloudFormation) and solving Cloud cost optimization challenges.
*   Representing the team to negotiate and sign a contract with a third-party provider to establish the official domain (`minisocial-network.id.vn`).

Through this journey, I have significantly improved my **Hard skills**, including AWS Architecture Design, IaC Deployment, CI/CD Management, and Cloud Cost Optimization. Simultaneously, my **Soft skills** were sharply refined, particularly in Leadership, Team Coordination, and Cross-functional Communication.

### Areas for Improvement

Although the project achieved significant success, I am deeply aware of the core barriers holding back my personal development:

*   **Personal Discipline:** Lacking strictness with myself. I need to establish a solid framework, strictly adhere to time management, respect working hours, and uphold personal commitments to the organization.
*   **Reading English Technical Documentation:** In the Cloud/AWS ecosystem, all the latest documentation and Best Practices are entirely in English. My limitations in reading native materials slow down knowledge acquisition and the debugging process. This is a crucial survival skill I must practice and improve daily.
