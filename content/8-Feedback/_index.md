> In this section, I would like to freely share my personal opinions and reflect on my entire experience participating in the **Bootcamp First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)**. The evaluations below are drawn from the practical perspective of someone who took on the role of Team Leader for a cross-functional team (Dev, Security, Network).

### Overall Assessment

**1. Working Environment & Community**  
The FCAJ community is truly a vibrant, friendly, and energetic academic environment. The continuous sharing of AWS architectures and knowledge kept my learning momentum high. 
Most importantly, this environment made me realize the **power of cross-functional collaboration**. Instead of struggling to learn security tools by myself, I simply asked the Security member in my team to use OWASP ZAP (DAST/SAST) to scan the backend for vulnerabilities. This coordination was highly practical and thoroughly optimized our time.

**2. Support from Mentors / Team Admins**  
The experience of working with FCAJ mentors and team admins was absolutely fantastic. The support team responded almost instantly whenever our group hit technical "roadblocks." The accurate assessments and constructive feedback from the mentors contributed significantly to shaping and perfecting the project's final architecture.

**3. Alignment between Work and Academic Major**  
Working directly with Cloud infrastructure and the 3-Tier architecture **completely shattered my previous theoretical boundaries**. Before joining FCAJ, to host a project, I often struggled with renting traditional VPS or using pre-built platforms like Render or Vercel. Thanks to the program, I have mastered AWS, making the process of deploying a complex system to a Production environment much more systematic, easier, and enterprise-standard.

**4. Learning Opportunities & Skill Development**  
* **Hard skills:** I am most satisfied with honing my DevOps mindset, especially the skill of Infrastructure as Code (IaC) automation by writing YAML files to deploy on AWS CloudFormation.
* **Soft skills:** The ability to **manage cross-functional personnel** was my biggest leap. Initially, I had to proactively research what students from other majors (Network, Security) actually study and how they do their projects. That understanding allowed me to maximize each person's strengths and keep the project running smoothly.

**5. Culture & Team Spirit**  
Team members were always ready to "back up" each other's work. However, as a Leader, my motto was: **Plan meticulously to minimize the need for "firefighting."** Through careful calculation and reasonable time allocation, I ensured that everyone only needed to do their specific tasks and meet their own deadlines for the whole system to integrate perfectly. As a result, despite the massive scale of the project, the team members rarely felt pressured or overloaded.

**6. Policies / Benefits for Interns**  
The **$200 AWS Credits** sponsorship from the organizers was truly a gift "worth its weight in gold." Although the team tried to design the most cost-optimized architecture, a real-world Cloud system always requires maintenance costs. Thanks to these credits, I had a perfect Sandbox to deploy, operate, and test a real system at almost zero personal cost.


### Other Reflections

- **Proudest Moment during the Internship:** It was when a team member asked me: *"Why is it that I do my work, you do your work, everyone just follows their own deadlines, yet by the end of the week's report, everything in the system fits together so perfectly?"*.
That question made me extremely proud of my budding team management skills. Even though it was just managing 5 people, that smooth coordination served as a massive stepping stone, giving me the confidence to aim for Tech Lead positions in the future.

- **Regarding the Learning Material Barrier:**
Even though AWS and the FCAJ program provided a lot of high-quality Vietnamese documentation, the AWS ecosystem is endless. Personally, I realized that forcing myself to read native English documentation is a mandatory path to survive and grow rapidly in the Cloud industry.


### Proposals & Desires

- **Suggestions for Improvement:** I hope that in upcoming Bootcamps, FCAJ can organize short orientation sessions to help students (especially non-Cloud majors) get accustomed to reading English Documentation early on, which will make them less overwhelmed when debugging systems.

*Finally, I would like to express my deepest gratitude to the Organizers, Mentors, and Team Admins of FirstCloudAIJourney for creating such a practical, professional, and valuable playground.*
